# Brand Perception and Sentiment Analysis of Insurance Companies

# Project Overview

This project explores consumer sentiment and brand health within the insurance industry by analyzing social media discourse and customer feedback. In a sector where trust is the primary commodity, understanding public perception is critical for identifying service pain points and competitive advantages. By leveraging Natural Language Processing (NLP), this study moves beyond simple volume metrics to uncover the emotional context and drivers behind brand mentions.

# Key Features
1. Unstructured Data Processing: Specialized cleaning and preprocessing of raw textual data to remove noise while preserving semantic meaning.
2. Sentiment Classification: Categorization of customer feedback into positive, negative, and neutral sentiments to quantify brand health.
3. Multi-Brand Mapping: Comparative analysis across competing insurance firms to visualize relative market positioning.
4. Pain Point Identification: Deep-dive keyword analysis focusing on critical service areas like claims processing and customer support.

# My Contributions

In this project, I spearheaded the end-to-end data pipeline, beginning with the comprehensive cleaning and preprocessing of unstructured textual data gathered from TrustPilot. I implemented sentiment classification models to accurately categorize customer feedback into distinct emotional states, allowing for a nuanced understanding of public opinion. Additionally, I developed comparative visualizations to map brand perception across competing firms and conducted deep-dive keyword analysis to pinpoint specific operational pain points, such as delays in claims processing and customer support friction.

# Methodology

The analytical workflow follows a rigorous sequence:

1. Data Acquisition: Gathering textual data from diverse insurance-related feedback channels.
2. Preprocessing: Applying NLP techniques including tokenization, stop-word removal, and normalization.
3. Sentiment Scoring: Utilizing polarity and subjectivity scoring to quantify the "temperature" of brand mentions.
4. Comparative Visualization: Mapping frequency distributions and sentiment across different insurance entities to identify outliers and trends.

# Tools Used

1. Languages: Python
2. Data Manipulation: Pandas, NumPy
3. NLP Libraries: NLTK, TextBlob
4. Visualization: Matplotlib, Seaborn
